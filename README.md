# Road to GM

A complete chess training app that runs entirely in your browser. No installs, no accounts, no internet needed. Built to do one thing: raise your rating until the day you can stare down Magnus on chess.com.

**Play it now: https://zeeshanejaz786.github.io/road-to-gm/**

## Run it locally

Double-click `index.html`. That is the whole setup.

For development with live reload tooling: `node server.js` and open http://localhost:4173.

## What is inside

**First Steps (Basics)**. Never played chess in your life? Start here. Eighteen hands-on lessons that assume nothing: what the squares are called, how every piece moves (you drag them around and collect stars), how capturing works, what check, checkmate, stalemate, and draws actually mean, plus castling, promotion, and en passant. It ends with a guided first game where the coach explains any piece you touch, warns you about check in plain words, and cheers you on like a kind teacher. New visitors are asked on first launch whether they want this path.

**The Ladder (Play)**. Seven rated bots from Pip (350) to Magnus Mode (2500). Pick your color, add a clock if you want (5, 10, or 15|10), and play rated games. Your Elo updates with the real formula (K=40 for your first 15 games, then 24). Beat a bot three times in a row, then move up.

**The Coach**. With coach mode on, every move you play is graded like chess.com does it: Best, Excellent, Good, Inaccuracy, Mistake, Blunder. Blunders come with an explanation, the move you should have played, and an offer to take it back so you learn on the spot. A live eval bar tracks the position, and each game ends with an accuracy score.

**Pass & Play**. Two humans, one board, with optional auto-rotate.

**Tactics**. 34 checkmate puzzles in three tiers (mate in 1, 2, and 3). Every puzzle was mined from engine self-play and then proof-checked: the winning first move is unique and the mate is forced. Wrong tries reset the position; streaks are tracked.

**Openings**. 28 lines from the Italian Game to the Najdorf, each with the idea behind it. Step through any line on a board, then drill it live against a bot that follows the book while you stay in it.

**The Path (Learn)**. A rating-band roadmap (what to actually train at 600, 1000, 1400, 1800, 2200+), the move-by-move thinking checklist, opening principles, a tactics glossary, and endgame survival rules.

**Your Story (Stats)**. Rating graph, peak, win/loss records per bot, accuracy trend, puzzle streaks. Everything persists in localStorage.

**Coins**. Every win pays out by opponent strength (beating Magnus Mode pays 100, plus 20 for your first win of the day; even a loss pays 2 for showing up). First-time puzzle solves and every fifth streak pay too. Spend coins on hints (5 each), board themes, and avatars. The Goat avatar can only be bought after you actually beat Magnus Mode.

## How to use it to actually improve

1. Play rated games with the coach on, against the bot flagged "your level".
2. Review every game. Click through the move list, find the worst move, understand the better one.
3. Ten puzzles a day. Streaks are motivation, patterns are the point.
4. One opening a week from the study room, drilled until the moves are automatic.
5. Move up the ladder only after three straight wins. Rating follows honesty.

## Under the hood

Pure vanilla JavaScript, zero dependencies, no build step.

- `js/engine.js` - 0x88 board representation, full rules (castling, en passant, promotion, threefold repetition, fifty-move rule, insufficient material), zobrist hashing, SAN, FEN.
- `js/ai.js` - hand-written alpha-beta search with transposition table, quiescence search, null-move pruning, late move reduction, and check extensions. Tapered evaluation with piece-square tables, pawn structure, king safety, and rook activity. Bot personalities layer noise and blunder rates on top to hit their target strengths.
- `js/coach.js` - move classification, accuracy from win-percentage swings, Elo math.
- `js/openings.js` - book trie and opening detection. Every line is test-verified legal.
- `js/puzzles-data.js` - generated by `tests/mine-puzzles.js`, engine-verified.

## Verification

Run from the project root (Node required):

- `node tests/perft.js full` - move generation against the standard perft suite, 26 checks up to depth 5 (4.86 million positions from the start position alone). 26/26 pass.
- `node tests/ai.test.js` - rules edge cases (en passant pins, castling rights on rook capture, stalemate, threefold) plus tactical checks (finds forced mates, refuses to hang the queen). All pass.
- `node tests/openings.test.js` - every book line replayed move by move through the engine. All pass.
- `node tests/mine-puzzles.js [seconds]` - regenerates the puzzle set with uniqueness proofs.
- `node tests/basics.test.js` - proves every First Steps lesson is solvable: star squares reachable, drill captures completable, mates present, special moves available. All pass.

## Honest notes

- Bot Elo labels are calibrated estimates, not certified ratings. Magnus Mode searches about 3 seconds a move and plays at strong master level, which is plenty to grow against.
- The coach analyzes for about a second per move, so its centipawn numbers are good but not gospel. The verdict tiers are wide enough that this rarely matters.
- Real improvement also needs humans: once you clear 1200 here, play rated games on chess.com or lichess alongside this. Bring the checklist with you.
